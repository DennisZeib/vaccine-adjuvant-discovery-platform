
Plain-English summary of findings

Date: 2026-06-05

What we ran
- Substrate sweep for the top 5 microbe pairs (glucose, acetate, lactate, glycerol), 48 h runs.
- Expanded sweep across glucose concentrations (10, 20, 50, 100 mM) and inoculum biomass ratios (1:1, 1:5, 1:20).

Top microbe pairs tested
- staphylococcus + bordetella
- clostridium + legionella
- salmonella + clostridium
- clostridium + helicobacter
- clostridium + vibrio

Key successful findings (plain English)
- Only glucose produced measurable LPS (polysaccharide_lps) for these five pairs. Acetate, lactate and glycerol produced essentially zero LPS in the tested runs.
- Highest instantaneous peak values (model output) occurred at **100 mM glucose** with inoculum ratio **1:20 (A:B)**. Example best run (all five pairs produced the same peak in the simplified model):
	- peak LPS ≈ 122.02 mM (instantaneous model peak)
	- peak time ≈ 0.2 hours (very early)
	- final LPS at 48 h ≈ 1.01 mM (integrated/steady result is much smaller than the instantaneous spike)

Important note about the peaks
- The extremely large, early peaks (e.g., 122 mM at 0.2 h) are numerical/model artifacts caused by the simplified kinetics in `micromol_core.py` (high reaction Vmax, small time step, and per-step decay). These spikes do NOT reflect realistic fermentation yields. Use integrated yield or a harvest window (e.g., integrated LPS over 0–2 h or the time to reach 95% of peak) rather than the instantaneous maximum.
